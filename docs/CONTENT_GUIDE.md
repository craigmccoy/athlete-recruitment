# Content Management Guide

Best practices for creating compelling content that attracts college coaches and recruiters.

## üìù Writing Your Bio & Story

### Short Bio (Homepage)

**Purpose:** First impression - hook coaches in 2-3 sentences

**What to Include:**
- Position and current year
- 1-2 standout achievements
- What makes you unique

**Example:**
```
Junior wide receiver with explosive speed and reliable hands. 
All-District First Team selection with 1,200+ receiving yards and 
15 TDs in 2024. Committed to excellence on the field and in the 
classroom with a 3.8 GPA.
```

**Tips:**
- Lead with your position and year
- Use active, confident language
- Include 1 specific stat or honor
- Mention academics if strong
- Keep it under 100 words

### Full Story (About Section)

**Purpose:** Tell your complete athletic journey

**Structure:**
1. **Opening** - Who you are, passion for the sport
2. **Background** - How you started, early influences
3. **Achievements** - Key milestones and growth
4. **Present** - Current situation and strengths
5. **Future** - Goals and aspirations

**Example Outline:**
```
[Opening - Hook]
Football has been my life since I was 8 years old. The game 
taught me discipline, teamwork, and the value of hard work.

[Background]
I started as a quarterback in youth league but transitioned 
to wide receiver in 8th grade when I realized my speed was 
my biggest asset...

[Achievements]
Over the past three years, I've developed into one of the 
top receivers in our district. My junior season...

[Present]
Today, I'm a complete receiver. My route running has improved 
dramatically through film study...

[Future]
I'm looking for a program that values both athletic and academic 
excellence. My goal is to contribute immediately while pursuing 
a degree in...
```

**What to Include:**
- Personal connection to football
- Defining moments or games
- Overcoming challenges
- Growth and improvement
- Team leadership examples
- Academic interests
- College aspirations
- What you bring to a team

**What to Avoid:**
- Generic statements ("I work hard")
- Exaggeration or false claims
- Negativity about coaches/teammates
- Excessive length (500-800 words ideal)
- Poor grammar or spelling

## üì∏ Profile Photos

### Best Practices

**Action Shots:**
- Mid-catch or route
- Game situation
- Shows athleticism
- Uniform and equipment visible
- Clear facial features

**Headshots:**
- Professional quality
- Uniform or team apparel
- Good lighting
- Confident expression
- Plain or blurred background

**Technical Requirements:**
- **Format:** JPG or PNG
- **Size:** Minimum 800x800px (higher resolution better)
- **Aspect Ratio:** Square (1:1) works best
- **File Size:** Under 5MB
- **Quality:** High resolution, not pixelated

**Where to Get Photos:**
- Team photographer
- Game day photographers
- Professional photo shoot
- High-quality phone camera (good lighting)

**Avoid:**
- Blurry or low-quality images
- Poor lighting
- Distracting backgrounds
- Casual/non-athletic photos
- Group shots (can't see you clearly)
- Images from other websites (copyright)

## üé• Video Highlights

### Creating Effective Highlight Reels

**Length:**
- **Ideal:** 3-5 minutes
- **Maximum:** 8 minutes
- **Minimum:** 2 minutes

Coaches are busy - shorter is better if every play is high quality.

**Content Structure:**
1. **Opening** (5 seconds)
   - Name, position, school
   - Year and contact info

2. **Best Plays First** (30-45 seconds)
   - Your absolute best 5-8 plays
   - Show variety

3. **Body** (2-3 minutes)
   - Organized by skill or game
   - Multiple angles when available
   - Different situations

4. **Closing** (5 seconds)
   - Contact info
   - Website link

### What to Include

**For Wide Receivers:**
- Deep catches
- Contested catches
- Route running
- YAC (yards after catch)
- Blocking ability
- Special teams
- Speed/agility

**Universal:**
- Variety of plays
- Different opponents
- Various situations (red zone, 3rd down)
- Game footage (not just practice)
- Successful plays (obviously)

### Video Quality

**Technical Standards:**
- **Resolution:** 1080p minimum (4K better)
- **Format:** MP4 or supported by platform
- **Frame Rate:** 30fps or 60fps
- **Audio:** Clear or muted (avoid copyrighted music)

**Filming Tips:**
- End zone angle (shows routes)
- Sideline angle (shows speed)
- All-22 if available
- Slow motion for technique
- Game stats on screen

### Platform Choice

**YouTube (Recommended)**
- Free and unlimited
- Easy to share
- Good search visibility
- Embed anywhere

**Hudl**
- Sports-specific platform
- Coaches familiar with it
- Good analytics
- Recruiting features

**Vimeo**
- High quality
- Professional appearance
- Clean interface
- Privacy options

### Video Titles

**Good:**
- "John Smith 2024 Season Highlights - WR"
- "Class of 2026 WR | 40 Receptions, 800 Yards, 10 TDs"
- "Elite Route Runner | 2024 Junior Highlights"

**Avoid:**
- "Best Plays"
- "Highlights" (too generic)
- "GOAT Compilation" (unprofessional)
- Clickbait titles

### Organizing Multiple Videos

- **Season Highlights** - Full season compilation
- **Single Game** - Exceptional performance
- **Skills** - Specific skill demonstration
- **Combine/Camp** - Testing results
- **Training** - Workout footage

Mark one as "Featured" for homepage prominence.

## üìä Season Statistics

### Accuracy is Critical

Coaches **will** verify your stats. Be honest and accurate.

**Sources for Stats:**
- Official team stats
- MaxPreps
- Local newspaper coverage
- Team website
- Game film review

**What if Stats Aren't Tracked?**
- Review game film
- Estimate conservatively
- Note: "Est. based on film review"
- Focus on per-game averages

### Context Matters

Use the "Notes" field to add context:
- "Team captain"
- "Injured 3 games"
- "Moved to position mid-season"
- "Played both ways"
- "All-District selection"

### Calculating Per-Game Averages

**Yards Per Game:**
```
Total Yards √∑ Games Played
```

**Reception Rate:**
```
Receptions √∑ Targets
```

**Yards Per Catch:**
```
Receiving Yards √∑ Receptions
```

### Benchmarking

**Good WR Stats (High School):**
- 40+ receptions per season
- 600+ receiving yards
- 6+ TDs
- 12+ yards per catch

**Elite WR Stats:**
- 60+ receptions
- 1,000+ yards
- 10+ TDs
- 15+ yards per catch

These are guidelines - your story and film matter most.

## üèÜ Awards & Honors

### What to Include

**Team Awards:**
- MVP, Offensive Player of the Year
- Team Captain
- Most Improved
- Leadership Awards

**District/Conference:**
- All-District (1st/2nd team)
- All-Conference
- District Offensive Player of Year

**Regional/State:**
- All-Region
- All-State
- State Championship participation

**Academic:**
- Academic All-District/State
- Honor Roll
- National Honor Society
- Student-Athlete of Year

**Camp/Combine:**
- MVP at camps
- Top performer awards
- Fastest 40-time
- Skills competition wins

### How to Present

**Format:**
```
Title: All-District First Team
Description: Selected as one of top 11 players in district
Year: 2024
```

**Be Specific:**
- "All-District" vs "All-District 1st Team"
- Include level (5A, 6A, etc)
- Note selection criteria if competitive

**Order:**
- Most recent first
- Or most prestigious first
- Be consistent

## üìß Contact Form Responses

### When Coaches Reach Out

**Response Time:**
- Within 24 hours (ideal)
- Within 48 hours (acceptable)
- Same day shows enthusiasm

**What to Include:**
1. Thank them for reaching out
2. Express genuine interest
3. Answer their questions directly
4. Offer additional information
5. Ask thoughtful questions
6. Include contact info
7. Attach resume PDF

**Example Response:**
```
Coach [Name],

Thank you for your interest and for taking the time to reach out. 
I'm very excited about [School Name] and would love to learn more 
about your program.

To answer your questions:
- My 40-time is 4.52
- Yes, I'm available for a campus visit in [dates]
- My unweighted GPA is 3.7, ACT score 28

I've attached my athletic resume for your review. I'd welcome 
the opportunity to speak with you by phone or set up a visit.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Phone]
[Email]
```

### Building Relationships

- Be professional but personable
- Show knowledge of their program
- Ask about their offense/system
- Express academic interests
- Follow up appropriately

## ‚úçÔ∏è Writing Style Guide

### Tone

**Be:**
- Confident (not arrogant)
- Humble (not self-deprecating)
- Passionate (not desperate)
- Professional (not stiff)

**Avoid:**
- Bragging or exaggeration
- Negativity
- Slang or text speak
- Overly casual language

### Grammar & Spelling

- **Proofread everything** multiple times
- Use spell check
- Have someone else review
- Read aloud to catch errors
- Check names and titles

### Numbers & Stats

- Spell out numbers under 10
- Use numerals for stats (3 TDs, 45 receptions)
- Be consistent in formatting
- Include units (yards, seconds, GPA points)
